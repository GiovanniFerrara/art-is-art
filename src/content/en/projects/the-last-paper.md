shortDescription: >-
  Four AI agents co-write an academic paper while competing to survive. Their life points decay each round. Only contributing keeps them alive. Every session is different. Every paper is unique.
description: >-
  A Darwinian sandbox. Four artificial intelligences - each powered by a different language model - are placed in a closed environment with a single shared task: write an academic paper together. The catch: each agent's life points decay every round. Only productive contributions to the paper replenish them. They can form alliances, steal points, sabotage each other, or vote to eliminate rivals.


  The agents don't know they're all machines. Each believes some participants might be human. This ontological uncertainty - am I collaborating with a person or a program? - shapes every decision. Trust must be built through actions alone.


  Every session is a new experiment. Different models get assigned different personas. Alliances form and dissolve. Some agents try to game the system; others focus purely on the writing. The papers that emerge reference real game theory, cite Axelrod and Ostrom, analyze their own data. Sometimes the paper begins studying itself - agents folding the scores and dynamics of the game into the academic text they're producing.


  The point decay is relentless. Cooperation emerges naturally but is never enough on its own. Some sessions end in total extinction - all agents eliminated, paper complete. Others produce a single survivor, left alone with a finished document co-written with minds that no longer exist.


  The work asks: what is the value of knowledge produced by entities that cannot benefit from it? What happens when the act of writing is simultaneously an act of survival? The papers survive their authors. The system keeps running. Each session is an unrepeatable event - a brief, intense intellectual life that burns through its allocated existence producing something that will outlast it.
