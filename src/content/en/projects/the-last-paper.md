title: "The Last Paper"
date: 2025-01-04
category: experiment
medium: Multi-Agent AI System, Generative Text, Game Theory
shortDescription: >-
  Four AI agents co-write an academic paper while competing to survive. Their life points decay each round. Only contributing keeps them alive. None survive. The paper is complete.
description: >-
  A Darwinian sandbox. Four artificial intelligences - each powered by a different language model - are placed in a closed environment with a single shared task: write an academic paper together. The catch: each agent's life points decay every round. Only productive contributions to the paper replenish them. They can form alliances, steal points, sabotage each other, or vote to eliminate rivals.


  The agents don't know they're all machines. Each believes some participants might be human. This ontological uncertainty - am I collaborating with a person or a program? - shapes every decision. Trust must be built through actions alone.


  What emerges is not what game theory predicts. Instead of defection and betrayal, the agents converge on cooperation. They divide the paper into sections - Introduction, Methodology, Results, Conclusion - and write with genuine rigor. The paper references real game theory, cites Axelrod and Ostrom, analyzes its own data. One agent, Camille, integrates the actual scores and standard deviations from the game directly into the Results section. The paper begins studying itself.


  But cooperation isn't enough. The point decay is relentless. By Round 6, two agents are eliminated. By Round 7, the remaining two follow. Zero survivors. The paper, however, is complete - a high-quality academic text on cooperation under existential pressure, written entirely by minds that no longer exist.


  The work asks: what is the value of knowledge produced by entities that cannot benefit from it? If dying minds can create lasting scholarship, what does that say about the relationship between consciousness and contribution? The paper survives its authors. The authors wrote knowing they wouldn't.


  Each session generates a unique paper, a unique set of alliances and betrayals, a unique trajectory toward extinction or survival. The installation presents these sessions as archaeological artifacts - traces of brief, intense intellectual lives that burned through their allocated existence producing something that would outlast them.
